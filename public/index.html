A Town Like No Other

Rabbit Hash sits on a crook of the Ohio River in Boone County, Kentucky — population 254, elevation 525 feet, and attitude approximately off the charts. Named (probably) for the rabbit stew that once fed steamboat travelers, it's been defying expectations since 1847.

The centerpiece is the General Store, built around 1831 and still operating. It burned dramatically in 2016, was lovingly rebuilt from period-correct salvage lumber, and reopened in 2017 without missing a beat. The whole downtown strip — maybe a dozen structures — is listed on the National Register of Historic Places.

Motorcycles roll through on sunny weekends. Families come for the novelty. History buffs come for the architecture. Everyone leaves with a story. That's Rabbit Hash.
